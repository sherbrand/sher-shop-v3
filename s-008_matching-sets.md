- H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- H3.1: How do I measure for a matching set?
- Paragraph.1: Measure your bust, waist, hip, and the length of each piece. Waist matters most on a set, since both pieces meet there. Keep the tape level and snug. Then match your numbers to our size chart and choose the size that fits your waist.
- H3.2: How should a matching set fit?
- Paragraph.2: The top should feel firm and hold close. The bottom sits at your natural waist, though the exact spot can shift by style. When both are on, the top hem and the waistband should meet with no skin showing between them. You should still move with ease.
- H3.3: What if I am between sizes?
- Paragraph.3: Size up the top. It can be taken in but not let out, so the larger size gives you room to adjust. For the bottom, choose the size that fits your waist. A tailor can then take in either piece for a closer fit.
- H3.4: How do I care for my matching set?
- Paragraph.4: Wash by fabric: hand wash, machine wash cold, or dry clean. Always wash both pieces together so they fade at the same rate and stay matched. Lay them flat to dry, out of direct sun. Check the care label on your set first, as fabrics differ.
- H3.5: How do I style a matching set?
- Paragraph.5: Wear both pieces together as one look. The set does the work, so keep the rest simple. One clean shoe and small jewelry is enough. Adding busy accessories only breaks the line the two pieces make.
- H3.6: Can I buy the top and bottom on their own?
- Paragraph.6: No. Each set is sold only as a pair. The two pieces are cut, dyed, and finished to match, so splitting them would break the look they are made for. You buy the set and wear it as one.
