- H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- H3.1: How do I measure for a corset top?
- Paragraph.1: Measure your bust, waist, hip, and length. Bust matters most on a corset top, so get that one right. Keep the tape level and snug, not tight. Then match your numbers to our size chart and pick the size that fits your largest one.
- H3.2: How should a corset top fit?
- Paragraph.2: It should feel firm but never painful. The top edge sits flat against your chest with no gap, and the waist holds close with no space to pinch. You should still breathe and move with ease. If it hurts or leaves marks, size up.
- H3.3: What if I am between sizes?
- Paragraph.3: Size up. A corset top can be taken in but not let out, so the larger size gives you room to adjust. With a Lace Closure, you can cinch it further for a closer fit. With a Zip Closure, a tailor can take in the seams.
- H3.4: How do I care for my corset top?
- Paragraph.4: Hand wash it cold or take it to a dry cleaner. Skip the machine, since the spin can bend the boning. Lay it flat to dry, out of direct sun. Always check the care label on your piece first, as fabrics differ.
- H3.5: How do I style a corset top?
- Paragraph.5: Let the corset top lead. Keep the rest simple, with plain trousers, a straight skirt, or tailored denim. One clean shoe and small jewelry is enough. The top is the piece people notice, so give it room and let everything else stay quiet.
- H3.6: Should I choose a Lace or Zip Closure?
- Paragraph.6: Pick by preference, since the fit is the same either way. Choose Lace Closure if you want to adjust how firm it feels, or if a size change is likely. Choose Zip Closure if you want the easiest way in and out. Both give you the same shape.
