- H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- H3#1: How do I measure for a cocktail dress?
- Paragraph#1: Measure your bust, waist, and hip with a soft tape, plus the length you want. A dress has to fit all three points, so look at each one. Size on the largest number, since that is the tightest spot. Then match it to our size chart and pick that size.
- H3#2: How should a cocktail dress fit?
- Paragraph#2: A cocktail dress should skim your body, not grip it. It follows your shape while still letting you move and breathe. Look in the mirror for horizontal creases, since those mean it is too tight. A clean, smooth line all the way down is the fit you want.
- H3#3: What if I am between sizes?
- Paragraph#3: If you fall between two sizes, size up. A larger dress can be taken in, but a tight one cannot be let out. Have the waist taken in by a tailor for a sharp, custom line. Sizing up first gives you room to shape it just right.
- H3#4: How do I care for my cocktail dress?
- Paragraph#4: Hand wash your dress in cool water, or have it dry cleaned. Never wring satin, since it leaves creases you cannot press out. Instead, press the water out gently and hang it to dry. Always follow the care label inside, as it comes first.
- H3#5: How do I style a cocktail dress?
- Paragraph#5: Let the dress lead, then add one strong accessory to finish it. A bold earring or clutch is enough. Match your shoe to the hem: a heel for a long hem, a strappy sandal for a short one. Keep the rest quiet so the dress stays the star.
- H3#6: What do I wear a cocktail dress to?
- Paragraph#6: Wear a cocktail dress to dressy events that sit just below black tie. Think weddings, dinners, parties, and work galas. When an invite says cocktail attire, this is the dress it means. It is dressed up enough to feel special, but not as formal as a full gown.
