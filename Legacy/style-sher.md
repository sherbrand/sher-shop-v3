# Visual Style

Pairs with the brand file — read both. It owns voice, positioning, and audience.

For each section: fill it, or write **None yet.** (still open — decide in real designs), or **Refer to the brand file** (covered there).

## 1. Look & Feel
Concept — the aesthetic in one sentence ("warm editorial, photography-led"). Personality and Resemblance are copied from the brand file; it is the source of truth.
- `Concept` - Editorial corsetry under gallery light — warm, sculpted, unhurried; photography leads and the interface only frames it.
- `Personality` - Magnetic, Sensual, Feminine, Refined
- `Resemblance` - Cherie (sweet sensuality), Perfect Corset (bold sensuality), Gigi Hadid (off-duty model)

## 2. Colour
One line per colour: name, hex, and role ("page base", "body text", "interactive only"). Name the set and never go outside it.
- `background` - `#F2F2EC` — warm off-white base
- `surface` - `#E3E2DD` — alternate section background
- `primary` - `#A99D94` — sandy grey; the only interactive colour
- `dark` - `#504F4D` — charcoal; dark sections, footer, high-contrast surfaces
- `text` - `#665D55` — body text and headings
- `muted` - `#929290` — non-interactive icons, dividers, metadata
- `white` - `#FFFFFF` — on dark surfaces

Approved pairs — which text sits on which background and passes WCAG AA ("text on background", "white on dark").
- `text on background`
- `dark on background`
- `dark on surface`
- `white on dark`
- Avoid: `muted` as text, and any text on `primary` — primary fails AA as a fill; use it as accent or outline only.

## 3. Typography
One line per role — Display (headings), Body (reading / product copy), UI (nav, labels, buttons). Give the typeface, a size range or "None yet", and case/tracking ("uppercase", "sentence case, +4% tracking").
- `Display` - Cormorant Infant — size None yet; always uppercase; generous tracking; large and confident; headings only
- `Body` - Cardo — size None yet; sentence case; editorial serif; product names, prices, descriptions
- `UI` - Manrope — size None yet; sentence case; recedes; navigation, labels, buttons

## 8. Shape & Depth
Corner-radius character ("small, 4px"), flat vs layered, and shadow philosophy ("depth from tone, not shadow").
- Soft, small radius on interactive elements — refined, not playful. Flat, not layered; depth comes from imagery and tonal contrast, not shadow.

## 9. Motion
Motion character — speed and easing ("quick, ease-out, subtle") — and any motion to ban ("no bounce or overshoot").
- Restrained and unhurried — nothing bounces, springs, or overshoots.

## 10. Do's & Don'ts
Approved moves to encourage ("full-bleed imagery"), and anti-patterns to ban, including generic-AI tells ("no gradients", "no drop shadows").
- `Do` - Let one photograph fill the frame; keep UI to the margins. Warm neutrals only. Display large and uppercase. Motion that settles.
- `Don't` - Cool or blue-toned greys; heavy drop shadows; bouncy or springy motion; pill shapes; bright gradients; placeholder-sounding copy; generic-SaaS "clean modern" veneer.
